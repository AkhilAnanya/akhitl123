Introduction of Urbanization and Its Environmental Challenges:

Urbanization:

urbanization is the process by which an increasing preparation of a population comes ot live in towns and cities. It involves the growth and expansion of urban areas, often accomapained by the migration of people from rural to urban areas in search of better econamic opportunities , improved living standards and acces to services.

Introduction:

Introduction: Histrically, urbanization has been a key aspect of societal development , closely tied to industrialization and economic progress. as more people move to urban centers , cities evolve into hubs of economic , cultural , nad social activities. However this transformative process brings with it a set of environmental challenges that demand attention and solutions.

Impact of Urbanization on the Environment

Urbanization can have a significant impact on the environment, including increased greenhouse gas emissions, deforestation, and loss of biodiversity. It's important to take steps to mitigate these impacts and promote sustainable urban development.

Challenges of Urbanization

Urbanization is a process by which more and more people move from rural areas or countryside to urban areas such as cities and towns, leading to th e expansion and growth of cities and towns. While urbanization has occurred in the past, it is currently happening on a global scale as resources and facilities tend to concentrate more in cities and towns. At present, half of the worldwide population is living in cities, and by 2050, this number will increase to two-thirds of the world’s population.
